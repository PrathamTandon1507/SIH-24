Soft Tissue Cancer: Causes, Symptoms, and Diagnosis

Soft tissue cancer affects the tissues that connect, support, or surround other structures and organs of the body, such as muscles, tendons, fat, and blood vessels. Sarcomas are the most common type of soft tissue cancer. Symptoms vary depending on the location and size of the tumor.

"Strength in the Fight"

More Information

Risk factors for soft tissue cancer include genetic predisposition and previous exposure to radiation. Diagnosis may involve imaging tests, biopsies, and blood tests. Treatment often includes surgery, radiation, and chemotherapy depending on the stage and location of the cancer.

Symptoms

  • Unexplained lump or swelling
  • Pain or tenderness in the affected area
  • Restricted movement if the tumor is near a joint
  • Changes in the appearance or texture of the skin

Diagnosis

Diagnosis of soft tissue cancer typically involves:

  • Imaging tests such as MRI or CT scans to locate and assess the tumor.
  • Biopsy to examine tumor cells.
  • Blood tests to check for markers associated with soft tissue sarcomas.
  • Genetic testing if there is a suspected hereditary risk.
