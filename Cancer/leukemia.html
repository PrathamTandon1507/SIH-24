Leukemia: Causes, Symptoms, and Diagnosis

Leukemia is a type of cancer that affects blood-forming tissues, including the bone marrow and lymphatic system. It involves the production of abnormal white blood cells. There are different types of leukemia, including acute and chronic forms. Symptoms and treatment vary based on the type and stage of leukemia.

More Information

Leukemia risk factors include exposure to radiation, certain genetic disorders, and previous chemotherapy treatments. Early symptoms might include frequent infections, fatigue, and easy bleeding. Regular check-ups and prompt medical attention can improve outcomes.

Symptoms

  • Frequent infections
  • Easy bruising or bleeding
  • Fatigue or weakness
  • Weight loss or loss of appetite

Diagnosis

Diagnosis of leukemia typically involves:

  • Blood tests to detect abnormal white blood cells.
  • Bone marrow biopsy to examine the marrow for cancerous cells.
  • Genetic tests to identify specific mutations.
  • Imaging tests to assess the spread of leukemia.
