Colon Cancer: Causes, Symptoms, and Diagnosis

Colon cancer starts in the large intestine (colon) and is often preceded by polyps. Risk factors include age, family history, and lifestyle factors such as diet and smoking. Early detection through screening can significantly improve outcomes.

"Early Detection Saves Lives"

More Information

Risk factors for colon cancer include a diet high in red meat, smoking, obesity, and family history. Regular screening, especially for those over 50 or with a family history, is crucial. A balanced diet and regular exercise may help lower risk.

Symptoms

  • Persistent changes in bowel habits
  • Abdominal pain or cramping
  • Blood in stool or rectal bleeding
  • Unexplained weight loss

Diagnosis

Diagnosis of colon cancer typically involves:

  • Colonoscopy to inspect the colon and remove polyps.
  • Biopsy of tissue samples obtained during colonoscopy.
  • Imaging tests such as CT scans to check for spread.
  • Blood tests to assess overall health and cancer markers.
