Kidney Cancer: Causes, Symptoms, and Diagnosis

Kidney cancer begins in the kidneys, with the most common type being renal cell carcinoma (RCC). Risk factors include smoking, obesity, high blood pressure, and family history. It can be asymptomatic in its early stages but may present with symptoms as it progresses. Early diagnosis improves treatment outcomes significantly.

More Information

Individuals with a family history of kidney cancer or genetic conditions like von Hippel-Lindau syndrome are at higher risk. Conditions such as chronic kidney disease and certain inherited genetic conditions also increase the likelihood of developing kidney cancer. Regular check-ups and monitoring are advised for those at risk.

Symptoms

  • Blood in the urine
  • Persistent back pain
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fever
  • Fatigue

Diagnosis

Diagnosis of kidney cancer typically involves:

  • Ultrasound imaging to detect kidney masses.
  • CT Scan for detailed images of the kidneys and surrounding tissues.
  • MRI to assess the extent and spread of the disease.
  • Liver Biopsy to confirm the presence of cancer cells.
