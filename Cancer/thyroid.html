Thyroid Cancer: Causes, Symptoms, and Diagnosis

Thyroid cancer occurs in the thyroid gland, located at the base of the neck. It may present as a lump or swelling in the thyroid area. The primary types include papillary, follicular, medullary, and anaplastic thyroid cancers. Papillary thyroid cancer is the most common, while anaplastic is the rarest and most aggressive. Risk factors include radiation exposure, family history, and certain genetic conditions. Treatment options vary based on cancer type and stage.

More Information

Risk factors include exposure to radiation, a family history of thyroid cancer, and certain genetic syndromes. Women are more likely to develop thyroid cancer than men. Early diagnosis is crucial as the cancer can grow slowly and may not cause symptoms in its early stages.

Symptoms

  • A lump or swelling in the neck
  • Hoarseness or changes in voice
  • Difficulty swallowing or breathing
  • Pain in the neck or throat area
  • Swollen lymph nodes in the neck

Diagnosis

Early detection is crucial for effective treatment. Common diagnostic methods include:

  • Thyroid Ultrasound to visualize nodules and their characteristics.
  • Fine Needle Aspiration Biopsy to obtain and analyze a sample from a thyroid nodule.
  • Thyroid Scan using radioactive iodine to detect abnormal growths.
  • Blood Tests to measure thyroid hormone levels and detect cancer markers.
